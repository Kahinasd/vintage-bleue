"Cinema is not just entertainment—it's a bridge between cultures, a mirror of society, and a voice for the voiceless."

Amine-Kais brings Algerian stories to global screens with authenticity, passion, and cinematic excellence.

Amine-Kais is an Algerian director, screenwriter, and producer who bridges cultures through powerful storytelling. With roots deeply planted in Algerian soil and experience gained in the American film industry, he creates works that resonate both locally and internationally.

"Facing myself, life holds challenges where cinema allows me to narrate unique stories of love, friendship, and betrayal.
I overcome time to live multiple lives, never succumbing to boredom, grief, or irresponsible love!"

The Journey

From Algiers to New-York and Back

His filmography includes critically acclaimed documentaries such as "Road to Freedom: Women in the Algerian Secret Services" (2012), "Men's Affair" (2008), and "Road to Freedom: The Radio Transmissions" (2013)—each reflecting a powerful Algerian identity carried by deeply engaging cinematic narrative.

After spending several years in the United States, Amine-Kais worked within the American film industry, where he refined his technical and artistic expertise. This dual influence gives his work a distinctive voice, blending cultural depth with global craftsmanship.

Hollywood Collaborations

International Recognition

Anthony J. Gallo took part in one of Amine Kais’s earliest film projects, starring in Men’s Affair (2008). This collaboration came during the actor’s early career, a time when he was building experience through various roles in independent films and television productions.

He later went on to share the screen with Robert De Niro, notably in productions such as The Alto Knights (2025), further establishing his place in the American film industry.

THE LARGEST DOCUMENTARY SERIES ON THE NATIONAL LIBERATION STRUGGLE

Abdelhafidh Boussouf, a nationalist militant born in 1926 in Mila, played a pivotal role in the Algerian Revolution. A former leader of the OS and a member of the FLN since 1954, he headed Wilaya V before founding and structuring the MALG (Ministry of Armaments and General Communications). This strategic ministry encompassed intelligence, counter espionage, communications, logistics, propaganda, and armaments. Boussouf established an organization inspired by the colonial army but tailored to the needs of the ALN. Under his leadership, hundreds of agents and operators were trained both in Algeria and abroad. The MALG supported both military operations and the diplomacy of the GPRA. His legacy includes the structuring of Algerian intelligence, military communications, armament networks, and the early foundations of the national air force.

TV Movie. 2002

Streets of Algiers

Crime / Action / Drama

The Streets of Algiers is a 2002 Algerian crime TV movie that follows the partnership between two police officers—an experienced veteran and a young, idealistic inspector—who are forced to work together. Through high-paced investigations, shootouts, and dangerous missions, a strong bond forms between them.
Set against the backdrop of Algiers, the film offers a dynamic and modern portrayal of the city’s streets, often overlooked or poorly depicted in cinema. Drawing inspiration from American classics like The Streets of San Francisco, it brings fast-paced action to Algerian television, with over 350 digitally-shot scenes, steady-car camera work, and the introduction of visual effects—a novelty in local productions of the time.
Aiming to break new ground in Algerian crime storytelling, this TV movie combines technical innovation with intense, urban action.

2008

Men's Affair

Drama / Action / Thriller

Men’s Affair is a gripping crime thriller that follows the story of an Algerian detective navigating complex investigations in the United States. Six years after his first TV film The Streets of Algiers (2001), the director shifts the action overseas, blending elements of espionage, personal history, and international intrigue.


The film draws strong inspiration from American crime cinema of the 1970s and reflects the filmmaker’s personal connection to intelligence and police stories, deeply rooted in family legacy and national history. Praised for its suspenseful plot and cinematic ambition, Men’s Affair marked a significant step in the director’s career, catching the attention of networks like Canal+ Overseas.

Maybe one day

Documentary

"Maybe One Day" presents the film "Nahla," directed by Farouk Beloufa, as recounted by Larbi Nasri, a young Algerian journalist, just after the Battle of Kfar Chouba in Lebanon in January 1975.

Immersed in the turmoil preceding the civil war, the documentary pays homage to Farouk Beloufa's work and traces the tumultuous history of the film's production, filled with poignant anecdotes. From the gripping moments of preparing for filming amidst the Lebanese war to the final editing of the work, it offers a striking glimpse into the artistic and human commitment of the team.

Driven by the performance of Youcef Saiah, the lead actor in Farouk Beloufa's work, the documentary explores the unbreakable bond between the artist and his director, as well as their collaboration with the Algerian technical team, including sound engineer Kamel Mekesser and cinematographer Allel Yahiaoui. Ahmed Bedjaoui, a pivotal figure, delves 45 years later into the depths of Farouk Beloufa's soul, thereby revealing the genius and artistic legacy of a visionary filmmaker.
